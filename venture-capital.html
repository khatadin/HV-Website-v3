Private Markets

Early-Stage
Venture Capital

HighVista's venture capital strategy provides access to concentrated portfolios of access-constrained, early-stage fund managers positioned to capitalize on defining secular trends in technology and life sciences.

Strategy Overview

Accessing the Most
Constrained Venture Funds

Consistent with HighVista's broader philosophy of targeting structurally inefficient and access-constrained markets, our venture capital strategy focuses on a specific and highly attractive segment: the best early-stage fund managers whose funds are consistently oversubscribed and difficult for most investors to access.

The strategy invests in a concentrated portfolio of these access-constrained managers — firms with exceptional track records in seed and Series A investing, deep networks in their target sectors, and fund sizes that maintain the discipline and flexibility needed to generate top-tier venture returns.

HighVista's venture capital team joined the firm in 2023 as part of the acquisition of the abrdn U.S. private markets business, bringing a seasoned team with deep relationships in the early-stage venture ecosystem and a history of securing allocations to the most sought-after managers.

In 2025, the team raised $270 million for its latest fund — oversubscribed — demonstrating strong investor confidence in HighVista's ability to deliver differentiated venture capital access.

01

Access-Constrained Managers

We focus specifically on early-stage fund managers whose funds are consistently oversubscribed — providing institutional investors with access that they could not achieve independently.

02

Concentrated, High-Conviction Portfolio

Rather than a broad fund-of-funds approach, we build concentrated portfolios of our highest-conviction manager relationships — typically 10–15 funds — to maximize the impact of access and selection.

03

Secular Trend Alignment

Portfolio managers are selected for their positioning in the most powerful secular trends: artificial intelligence, enterprise software, fintech, life sciences, and blockchain infrastructure.

04

HighVista Platform Advantage

Venture capital managers benefit from HighVista's broader platform — connections across private equity, private credit, and institutional networks that create a richer ecosystem for portfolio companies.

Target Sectors

Secular Trends Driving
Exceptional Returns

HighVista's venture portfolio managers are concentrated in sectors with durable structural tailwinds and the greatest potential for disruptive, venture-scale returns.

AI

Artificial Intelligence

Foundation models, AI infrastructure, and applied AI applications across industries represent the defining technology platform of this era. HighVista's managers have early, concentrated exposure to category-defining AI companies.

SaaS

Enterprise Software

Mission-critical software for businesses — from vertical SaaS to developer tools to AI-enhanced workflows — continues to produce durable, capital-efficient businesses with exceptional unit economics.

Bio

Life Sciences

Biotechnology, drug discovery, medical devices, and computational biology — sectors where HighVista has long-standing expertise through its public markets biotechnology team — continue to offer exceptional venture returns.

Fin

Fintech

Financial services technology — payments infrastructure, digital banking, insurance technology, and capital markets innovation — remains a deep well of venture opportunity driven by ongoing disruption of legacy financial systems.

∞

Blockchain & Web3

Decentralized infrastructure, tokenized assets, and blockchain-native applications represent an emerging technology stack where early fund-level access can yield exceptional risk-adjusted returns for patient investors.

+

Emerging Opportunities

HighVista's venture portfolio is not static — we continuously evaluate emerging sectors and manager relationships to ensure the portfolio remains positioned in the most attractive areas of the early-stage landscape.
